## Sound Design Brainstorm

To create a progressive house bassline, start with a simple but memorable melodic phrase. The progression should feel familiar yet fresh, drawing inspiration from classic prog house motifs while infusing modern touches. Aim for a BPM range of 118-128 to maintain the genre's characteristic energetic flow.

You can achieve this using a synth like Serum or Spire. Begin with a sine wave or sawtooth oscillator and apply some low-pass filtering to create a warm, smooth sound. Experiment with unison voices to thicken the bassline and add stereo width. Modulate the filter cutoff to introduce subtle movements that complement the track's arrangement sections.

For more intensity, consider adding minor distortion or overdrive effects after the filtering stage. This can help the bassline cut through the mix without sounding muddy. Remember, less is often more in progressive house; a few well-chosen notes can have a greater impact than a busy, cluttered bassline.

If you want to incorporate Reese bass elements, use a supersaw with detuned saw oscillators for a large, cavernous sound. Add some gentle automation to the filter cutoff to create subtle movements throughout the track. This technique works well in higher-energy sections of your progressive house track.

In summary:
1. Create a memorable melodic phrase using a sine wave or sawtooth oscillator.
2. Apply low-pass filtering and unison voices for warmth and thickness.
3. Modulate the filter cutoff to add subtle movement, or use supersaws with automation for Reese bass-inspired sounds in energetic sections.
4. Add minor distortion or overdrive effects after filtering to maintain clarity without sounding muddy.

Remember to adjust these ideas as needed to fit your specific track's arrangement, mood, and energy.
