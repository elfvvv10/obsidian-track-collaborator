Given your track style of progressive house, here are some compositional ideas for a bassline:

1. [Inference] Progressive house basslines often have a strong melodic element, combining musical phrases with rhythmic patterns. A classic approach is to use a repeating melodic motif that evolves throughout the arrangement, mirroring the song's structure.

2. Start your track with a simple, catchy bass riff that immediately conveys the style and energy of progressive house. You might use a synth bass or even an acoustic-sounding electric bass processed through effects.

3. [Local 1] As the track progresses to later sections, consider using more intricate and layered basslines. Add in additional notes, filters, distortion, and other processing to keep the listener engaged while building tension towards the drop.

4. For the buildup section before the final drop, use a bassline with a powerful and driving feel, often incorporating elements from earlier bass riffs or melodies to create a sense of familiarity and anticipation.

5. [Inference] When designing your basslines, think about how they can complement and interact with the other musical elements in your track, such as the melody, drums, and other instruments. A well-designed bassline should support and enhance the overall composition.
