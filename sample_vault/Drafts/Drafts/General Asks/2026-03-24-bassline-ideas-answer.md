## Answer

Based on the provided evidence, here are some ideas for creating basslines in drum and bass:

1. Use Reese basses: Reese basses are a staple in drum and bass, featuring longer, drawn-out notes with subtle movement over time. They can be created using a supersaw (detuned saw with many unison voices) playing a low note, multiple oscillators for thickness, filtering to move the bass during certain parts of the track, and optional distortion after filtering to add color.

2. Experiment with sound design: In deeper subgenres like Liquid and Minimal, basslines tend to use less distortion and upper harmonics, sometimes consisting only of a single sine wave for clean bass reproduction in clubs. Adding subtle saturation can warm up the sound without degrading high speaker performance. More intense genres may utilize more distortion and effects for energy, color, and interest.

3. Choose appropriate keys: Due to their well-reproduction on subwoofers and vibrational effect on human bodies, drum and bass basslines often occupy the sub-bass frequency range (75-100Hz downward). These basses are typically written in keys like E Minor, F Minor, or F# Minor.

Remember that there's no need to reinvent the wheel when it comes to drums. Utilize high-quality samples and search for existing sampled breaks online to save time and effort. Experiment with different frequencies, distortion levels, and effects to find the perfect sound for your track.
