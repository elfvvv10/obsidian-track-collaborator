## Answer

Based on the provided local and external evidence, here are some ideas for creating a progressive house bassline:

1. Use a clean sine wave or a supersaw (detuned saw with many unison voices) playing a low note to create a spacious and large feeling bass.

[Import 1]
The use of sine waves and supersaws can help achieve a clear, warm bass tone that is characteristic of progressive house. Adjusting the filtering and distortion can add further color and depth to the sound.

2. Add subtle amounts of saturation or distortion to warm up the bass sound, especially if you want it to cut through the mix on high speakers.

[Import 1]
A bit of saturation, like Ableton Live's 'A Bit Warmer' preset, can help thicken the bass without making it sound muddy. This is particularly useful in progressive house tracks where the bass needs to stand out.

3. Experiment with Reese basses by using longer, drawn-out bass notes with subtle movement over time for a more intense and energetic feel.

[Import 2]
Reese basses are commonly used in drum & bass but can work well in progressive house too. The longer notes and subtle movement create a sense of anticipation and build-up that is often found in the genre.

4. Consider incorporating elements from tech house and UK garage or drum & bass to add unique rhythmic influences to your progressive house track.

[Local 1]
Incorporating these influences can help differentiate your track from others and bring new life to a potentially tired structure. Remember, every section should justify its existence and contribute to the overall journey of the track.

When creating your bassline, focus on making it feel like part of the journey rather than just repeating the same pattern throughout the song. Experiment with different sounds, filters, and distortions to find what works best for your specific track. Don't be afraid to try new techniques or combine elements from various genres to create something unique and exciting.

[Inference]
By using a combination of these suggestions and tailoring them to your individual needs, you can create a progressive house bassline that is both engaging and effective in driving the energy of your track forward.
