Based on the provided local note context about creating basslines for drum & bass and a track critique framework for progressive house, here are some ideas for creating a progressive house bassline:

1. Utilize a combination of synths and samples: In progressive house, basslines often incorporate a mix of synth-generated tones and samples from acoustic instruments or other sources. Experiment with different sounds to find what works best for your track.

2. Add variation in rhythm and pitch: Progressive house basslines typically feature an evolving rhythm and pitch. Try incorporating varying note lengths, intervals, and dynamics within the bassline to keep listeners engaged and add interest to your track.

3. Integrate elements from related genres: As suggested by the track critique framework, consider drawing influences from other styles like tech house, UK garage, or drum & bass. These genres can provide inspiration for unique bass textures and patterns.

4. Use filtering and distortion creatively: Filtering can be used to shape the sound of your bassline, while distortion can add warmth and character. Experiment with different settings to achieve the desired effect without overdoing it.

5. Consider the overall track structure: As mentioned in the core philosophy section, every element should justify its existence within the track. Make sure your bassline contributes to the overall progression and mood of the song.

Remember that creating a successful progressive house bassline takes practice and experimentation. Listen to tracks you enjoy and try to identify the elements that make them stand out. Adjust and refine your ideas based on feedback and personal taste until you find what works best for your sound.
